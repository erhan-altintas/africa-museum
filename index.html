Reflecting on History

Our visit to the Royal Museum for Central Africa in Tervuren

The Assignment

As part of our cultural activity, our group visited the Royal Museum for Central Africa (AfricaMuseum) in Tervuren. The assignment tasked us with engaging in an educational activity centered around the dehumanization of a specific group of people.

We chose to learn more about Belgium's colonization of Congo. The museum, having been recently refurbished, takes a critical look at its own dark history and attempts to make amends with the people it previously helped to suppress.

About the Museum

The Royal Museum for Central Africa is a center for knowledge and resources on Africa, in particular Central Africa, in an international context. The museum aims to present a contemporary view of Africa, past and present.

Historically, the museum played a key role in colonial propaganda. Today, after a major renovation, it strives to decolonize its narrative and address the controversial aspects of Belgium's colonial past. It confronts visitors with questions about representation, memory, and the enduring impact of colonization.
